Investment Thesis

The maturation of DeFi requires a shift from retail-driven, high-risk yield farming to professional, risk-managed treasury operations. Currently, protocol treasuries, L2 foundations, and DAOs collectively manage billions in assets, yet lack the tooling to deploy this capital efficiently and safely. This creates a significant drag on capital efficiency and exposes ecosystems to systemic risks.

The next wave of DeFi growth will be unlocked by infrastructure that provides operational trust and policy-based automation for on-chain capital management.

Our thesis rests on three core assumptions:

  • Demand for Durability: Protocols will increasingly prioritize sustainable, long-term liquidity and yield over short-term, speculative APYs.
  • Operational Overhead is a Bottleneck: Manual treasury management is slow, error-prone, and does not scale. Automation via secure, auditable smart contracts is the only viable path forward.
  • Neutrality Wins: A neutral infrastructure provider that does not compete with its clients (e.g., by launching its own yield products) will become the trusted standard for liquidity routing and management.

Business Model

Gravitas Protocol operates on a transparent, volume-based fee model, supplemented by enterprise-grade services for larger partners. Our goal is to align our success directly with the capital efficiency we provide to our clients.

Revenue Streams

  • Core Protocol Fee: A basis point (bps) fee of 5-10 bps is charged on all volume routed through the protocol. This fee is programmatically collected and accrued to the protocol treasury.
  • Enterprise Add-ons: For large partners requiring dedicated infrastructure, bespoke policy modules, or priority support, we offer tailored service agreements with fixed retainers or tiered fee structures.
  • Future Token (Optional): Post-product-market fit, a token may be introduced for governance and potential fee-sharing, ensuring long-term alignment with the community. This is not part of the initial fundraising scope.

Unit Economics

The protocol is designed for high margins and scalability. Our primary costs are fixed (engineering, security), while revenue scales linearly with volume, leading to strong operating leverage.

  • Gross Margin: >85%. The primary cost of revenue is gas fees for automated rebalancing, which are minimized through efficient batching and L2 execution.
  • Support & Integration Cost: Minimal per-partner cost due to a standardized adapter architecture. New integrations are capital expenditures, not operational ones.
  • Leverage: Each new adapter (e.g., for Aave, Compound, Morpho) exponentially increases the number of possible strategies, creating compounding value for all users.

Moat (Defensibility)

Our long-term defensibility is built on network effects, operational trust, and governance neutrality.

  • Network of Integrations: Each new protocol adapter and partner integration strengthens the entire network, creating a flywheel effect. The deep, technical nature of these integrations creates high switching costs.
  • Operational Trust: In DeFi, trust is earned through uptime, security, and flawless execution. By achieving a track record of 99.9% uptime and 0 critical incidents, we become the default, trusted provider.
  • Governance-Safe Policies: Our rigid, on-chain policy engine and timelocks make us a "governance-safe" choice for DAOs. They can integrate Gravitas without granting a third party discretionary control over their treasury.

Risks & Mitigation

We identify two primary categories of risk: market/adoption and security/execution.

Market & Adoption Risk

  • Risk: Slow adoption by large protocols due to lengthy governance processes.
  • Mitigation: Focus on founder-led teams and L2 foundations initially, which have faster decision-making cycles. Build modular, easy-to-integrate solutions to reduce the burden on partner engineering teams.

Security & Execution Risk

  • Risk: Smart contract vulnerability in our core logic or adapters.
  • Mitigation: A multi-layered security approach: multiple independent audits from top-tier firms, a public bug bounty program, comprehensive test coverage (>95%), and on-chain monitoring with automated circuit breakers.

Governance & Compliance

The protocol is governed by a robust, security-first framework designed to protect user funds and ensure predictable, safe operation.

Security Posture

  • Multisig Control: Critical functions are controlled by a 3/5 Gnosis Safe multisig composed of the core team and trusted industry advisors.
  • Timelock: All critical changes (e.g., fee changes, new adapters) are subject to a 48-hour timelock, allowing partners to review and react to upcoming changes.
  • Guardian Role: A designated "Guardian" address has the power to pause the protocol in case of an emergency, but cannot withdraw funds or change parameters.

We are actively engaging with legal counsel to ensure compliance with evolving digital asset regulations in key jurisdictions.

Investor FAQ

How do protocol fees accrue?

Fees are programmatically swept into a dedicated multisig treasury controlled by the protocol. Initially, these funds will be used to cover operational expenses and fund further development. Future governance may decide on other uses.

Is custody of funds ever taken?

No. Gravitas is entirely non-custodial. Partner funds are managed via smart contract allowances and are always held in the partner's own contracts or in the underlying DeFi protocols (e.g., Aave aTokens).

Why would a partner choose Gravitas over building in-house?

Building and maintaining a secure, efficient, and audited liquidity management system is a significant undertaking that distracts from a protocol's core mission. We provide this as a specialized service, offering greater security and lower overhead than an in-house solution.

What is the policy on a future token?

A token is not required for the protocol to function or generate revenue. A token would only be introduced if it serves a clear purpose for decentralizing governance and aligning long-term incentives, and only after we have achieved significant product-market fit.

Contracts & Adapters Map

The core system comprises a central `PolicyEngine` contract that interfaces with various `Adapters` (e.g., `AaveV3Adapter`, `CompoundV2Adapter`). A `Registry` contract manages active adapters and policies.

Test Plan & Security Posture

  • Test Plan: Comprehensive suite using Foundry, with >95% code coverage, extensive fuzzing, and invariant testing.
  • Security Posture:
    • 3/5 Multisig, 48h Timelock, Guardian Role.
