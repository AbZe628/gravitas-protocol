Investment Thesis

The maturation of DeFi requires a shift from retail-driven, high-risk yield farming to professional, risk-managed treasury operations. Currently, protocol treasuries, L2 foundations, and DAOs collectively manage billions in assets, yet lack the tooling to deploy this capital efficiently and safely. This creates a significant drag on capital efficiency and exposes ecosystems to systemic risks.

Our thesis is that a neutral, non-custodial infrastructure layer that provides operational trust and policy-based automation for on-chain capital management will unlock the next wave of DeFi growth and institutional adoption.

Architecture Deep Dive

Gravitas is a modular, multi-layered system designed for security, extensibility, and operational resilience. The core logic is separated from external integrations, allowing for rapid development and isolated risk.

Sequence of Operations

  1. Policy Validation: A transaction is initiated, and the `PolicyEngine` verifies it against the treasury's registered policy constraints.
  2. Simulation Check: The transaction is dry-run against a mainnet fork to predict outcomes like slippage and gas costs, ensuring they are within acceptable bounds.
  3. Adapter Invocation: The `PolicyEngine` calls the appropriate, isolated `Adapter` contract to execute the transaction on the target DeFi protocol.
  4. Fee Sweep: A protocol fee (5-10 bps) is programmatically swept to the protocol treasury upon successful execution.
  5. Event Emission: A detailed log of the entire operation is emitted as an on-chain event for indexing and real-time monitoring.

Policy Constraints Framework

Each treasury can define a granular policy with rules enforced at runtime, including:

  • Asset Allowlist: A specific list of permissible assets for deposit and withdrawal.
  • Exposure Caps: Maximum allocation percentage per asset, protocol, or strategy.
  • Oracle Freshness Thresholds: Reverts if price data is older than a specified duration (e.g., 60 seconds).
  • Slippage Ceilings & Velocity Limits: Maximum permissible slippage on trades and limits on the value that can be moved within a given epoch.

Security & Assurance

Our security posture is built on a defense-in-depth model, assuming adversarial conditions at every layer. The protocol is designed to be failsafe, prioritizing the preservation of capital above all else.

Static Guards (Code-Level)

  • Immutable Logic: Core contracts are immutable, preventing unauthorized upgrades.
  • Standard Protections: Built-in safeguards against reentrancy, integer overflow/underflow, and non-zero address checks.
  • Adapter Isolation: A vulnerability in one adapter cannot affect the core engine or other adapters.

Dynamic Guards (Runtime)

  • Circuit Breakers: Automated triggers that can pause deposits to a specific asset or protocol if unusual activity is detected.
  • Volatility-Aware Slippage: Slippage tolerance is dynamically adjusted based on real-time market volatility of the underlying assets.

Governance & Testing

  • Governance: A 3/5 Gnosis Safe multisig controls non-critical parameters, with all changes passing through a 48-hour timelock. A separate Guardian role can pause the system but cannot withdraw funds or modify parameters.
  • Testing Regimen: Our process includes Foundry-based invariant and fuzz testing, maintaining >95% code coverage, mainnet-fork simulations, and a library of historical exploit replay tests.

Monitoring & Audit Plan

  • On-Chain Events: We emit detailed events for critical actions, including `FAILED_TX`, `CIRCUIT_BREAKER_TRIPPED`, `POLICY_BREACH_ATTEMPT`, and `FEE_CHANGE_PROPOSED`.
  • Audit Plan: One full internal review pass, followed by one external audit from a top-tier firm, and a formal verification engagement focused on the `PolicyEngine`'s core mathematical logic.

Operational Playbook

Reliability is paramount. We operate with a playbook that prioritizes transparency, predictability, and rapid, safe incident response.

  • Change Management: All protocol upgrades are queued via the timelock with on-chain announcements and human-readable diff previews.
  • Incident Response: A clear runbook for incident triage, activating Guardian pauses, stakeholder communication, and public postmortems.
  • Deployment Cadence: All new adapters and features are first deployed on an L2 testnet as a canary before being proposed for mainnet inclusion. Our primary SLO is 99.9% availability for all core services.

Data & Telemetry

The protocol is designed to be fully transparent and observable. All significant state changes and actions are logged as structured on-chain events.

  • Public Dashboards: We will provide public Dune dashboards for monitoring key metrics like total volume, fees generated, and adapter utilization.
  • SIEM Integration: A standardized webhook pattern is available for partners to ingest our on-chain event stream directly into their Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems.
  • Data Retention: On-chain events are retained indefinitely by the blockchain, providing a permanent, immutable audit trail.
