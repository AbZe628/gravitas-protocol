What we do
Deterministic, one-click migrations with strict guardrails (slippage, deadlines, whitelists).
Who we serve
DEXs, DAOs/Treasuries, funds & market makers who rebalance across venues/chains.
Why now
Liquidity rotates weekly; fragmentation increases. Execution needs to be safe, fast, and predictable.

Investment Thesis

We are building the neutral transport layer for liquidity — a category that compounds with market depth, partners, and integrations.

Thesis in one sentence

Liquidity flow is recurring and protocol-agnostic; capturing a bps-level toll on deterministic migrations is a defensible, high-margin business.

Key insight: execution certainty beats theoretical APR — desks pay for reliability.

Core assumptions

  • Venue proliferation and cross-chain rotation persist.
  • Institutions require auditability, policy controls, and rollback plans.
  • Agnostic infra wins partnerships faster than “walled-garden” UX layers.

Business Model

We earn bps on successful migrations and offer enterprise features on top.

Revenue streams

  • Protocol fees: 5–10 bps per migration (transparent, on-chain).
  • Enterprise add-ons: SLA support, SSO, policy templates, custom reporting.
  • Optional: fee-sharing and staking bonds if a token is introduced post-PMF.

Pricing rationale

For desks, failed execution is more expensive than fees. Our bps toll is a fraction of slippage avoided and time saved.

Investor FAQ

How do fees accrue?

Bps-level micro-fees are deducted on successful migrations and routed to the protocol Treasury with on-chain transparency.

Is there custody risk?

No. Gravitas orchestrates via whitelisted adapters and routers; funds travel through user-authorized flows.

Why will partners integrate?

We lift conversion and retention by removing migration friction and offering institution-grade controls and logs.

Will you issue a token?

Only post-PMF if it strengthens governance and long-term incentives (fee-sharing, staking bonds) without harming neutrality.
